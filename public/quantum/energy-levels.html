Quantum Energy Levels

In atoms, electrons can only exist at specific, discrete energy levels - they cannot exist between these levels. This is a fundamental principle of quantum mechanics known as energy quantization.

The energy of each level is given by: En = -13.6 eV / n¬≤, where n is the principal quantum number (1, 2, 3, ...).

When an electron absorbs energy (like from a photon), it jumps to a higher energy level (excitation). When it drops to a lower level, it releases the energy difference as a photon with a specific wavelength/color.

The energy of the emitted photon equals the energy difference: Ephoton = hŒΩ = Ehigh - Elow

This is why each element produces a unique emission spectrum - a fingerprint of specific wavelengths that can be used to identify elements, even in distant stars!
