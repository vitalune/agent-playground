The Thought Experiment

A cat is placed in a sealed box with a radioactive atom, a Geiger counter, and a vial of poison. If the atom decays, the Geiger counter triggers and releases the poison, killing the cat.

According to quantum mechanics, until we observe the system, the atom exists in a superposition of both decayed and not-decayed states. This means the cat is simultaneously both alive and dead.

Only when we open the box and observe does the wave function "collapse" into one definite state. The act of measurement forces reality to choose.

Key Insight: This paradox highlights the bizarre nature of quantum superposition and the role of observation in quantum mechanics. It questions where quantum rules end and classical physics begins.
